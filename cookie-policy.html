02

Cookies, Local Storage, and Similar Technologies

Browsers provide several ways for websites to remember information or deliver technical functionality.

Cookies

Cookies are small text files that a website or service provider may place on a device through a browser. Cookies can contain identifiers, preferences, session information, or other limited values used by a website.

Local Storage

Local storage is a browser feature that allows a website to retain information on the visitor’s device. Unlike a traditional cookie, local-storage information is not automatically included with every website request.

Session Storage

Session storage can retain information temporarily during a browser tab or session. It is generally removed when that tab or session ends, although browser behavior can vary.

Server and Security Records

Website requests may create server, hosting, security, content-delivery, or error records even when a browser does not accept optional cookies.

Embedded and External Resources

Fonts, scripts, icons, stylesheets, images, or similar resources may be loaded from an external provider. The request for such a resource may include ordinary technical information such as an Internet Protocol address, browser details, referring page, and request time.

First-Party Storage
Storage created for the Growwise Urban website and associated with the website’s own domain or browser context.
Third-Party Resource
A file, service, or page delivered by an independent external provider.
Essential Function
A function reasonably necessary for website delivery, security, preference handling, or basic operation.
Optional Technology
A technology that is not required for basic page delivery and may require a separate choice depending on its purpose and applicable law.

Purposes of Browser Storage and Technical Processing

Storage and technical records may support preference handling, security, communication, and ordinary website operation.

Browser storage and related technical information may be used to:

  1. Remember whether a visitor accepted or declined the cookie preference.
  2. Prevent the same cookie notice from appearing repeatedly after a preference has been stored.
  3. Deliver pages, images, scripts, fonts, icons, and stylesheets.
  4. Receive and validate contact-form requests.
  5. Detect spam, automated submissions, malicious requests, and unusual activity.
  6. Troubleshoot errors, broken resources, server problems, and browser compatibility.
  7. Maintain website security, stability, availability, and performance.
  8. Comply with legal obligations and protect legitimate rights.

Preference Memory

The cookie preference is stored so the website can remember your choice across pages and later visits from the same browser profile.

Security and Abuse Prevention

Technical records may be reviewed to investigate unusual request patterns, repeated failed submissions, automated spam, malicious payloads, excessive traffic, attempted unauthorized access, or other security concerns.

Website Administration

Error records and technical request information may help identify missing files, script failures, broken links, browser issues, server errors, and performance problems.

Essential Operation

Some technical processing occurs because a browser must communicate with web servers and resource providers to display a page, even when optional browser storage is declined.

05

Technical Records, Hosting Logs, and Form Requests

Ordinary website operation may create technical records independently of browser preference storage.

When your browser requests a website page or file, technical infrastructure may receive information such as:

  • Internet Protocol address.
  • Browser name and version.
  • Device and operating-system information.
  • Requested page, script, image, or file.
  • Date and time of the request.
  • Referring page or domain.
  • Response status and error information.
  • Approximate location inferred from network information.
  • Security, rate-limit, or abuse-detection information.

Contact-Form Requests

A contact-form submission creates a request to the website server. The request may contain the information entered into the form, technical validation values, source-page information, and ordinary server-request metadata.

Hidden Anti-Spam Field

The contact form may use a hidden field intended to identify automated submissions. A genuine visitor should leave that field empty.

Request Limits

The form processor may limit request size, validate expected fields, reject malformed content, sanitize submitted text, and apply other reasonable anti-abuse controls.

Technical Logs Are Not Always Cookies

Clearing cookies or local storage does not necessarily remove hosting, security, email, backup, or server records that were created when a request was processed.

Your Cookie and Browser Choices

You can manage website storage through the Growwise Urban preference interface and your browser settings.

Cookie Preference Interface

When the preference notice appears, you may be able to accept or decline optional website storage choices. The selected value may be stored under the key growwiseCookiePreference.

Changing a Saved Preference

You can remove the saved preference through your browser’s site-data, local-storage, cookie, or privacy settings. After removal, the preference notice may appear again on a later visit.

Browser Controls

Most browsers allow you to:

  • View stored cookies and local-storage values.
  • Delete storage for a specific website.
  • Clear all browsing data.
  • Block some or all cookies.
  • Restrict third-party cookies.
  • Use private or incognito browsing modes.
  • Control permission settings for individual websites.
  • Install privacy or content-blocking tools.

Effect of Blocking Storage

Blocking or deleting storage may cause the cookie notice to appear repeatedly, prevent the website from remembering your preference, or affect other browser-dependent functionality.

Do Not Track and Global Privacy Signals

Browsers and extensions may transmit privacy preference signals. Because technical standards and legal requirements continue to develop, the website’s response may depend on the signal, browser, provider, jurisdiction, and available implementation.

External Resource Blocking

Blocking external scripts, fonts, icons, or stylesheets may alter page appearance, disable interactive features, or prevent some visual resources from loading.

Browser Settings Vary

Menu names and available controls differ between browsers and devices. Review your browser provider’s current support documentation for exact instructions.
