Indoor-growing projects involve practical risks that remain the responsibility of the person planning and operating the setup.

Electricity and Heat

Nutrients and Other Products

Nutrients, pH products, cleaning solutions, pest-control products, test solutions, and other substances may require protective equipment, ventilation, accurate measurement, secure storage, and careful disposal.

Biological and Health Context

Plants, seeds, fungi, growing media, standing water, dampness, mold, spores, allergens, pests, microorganisms, contaminated crops, and uncertain biological growth may create health or sanitation concerns.

Edible Crops

You are responsible for crop identification, seed suitability, water, growing inputs, cleanliness, harvesting, washing, storage, cooking, allergy considerations, food safety, and deciding whether a crop is suitable for consumption.

Stop When Conditions Are Uncertain

Do not consume unidentified plants, mushrooms, sprouts, microgreens, damaged produce, contaminated crops, or material with uncertain safety. Seek appropriate qualified guidance when necessary.
